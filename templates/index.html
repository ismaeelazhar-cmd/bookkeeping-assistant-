Compound Journal Entry

For one payment or receipt that splits across several accounts (e.g. a BACS run covering Rent + Utilities + Insurance from one bank line). Pick the one account every line below shares — and add a line per other account. This posts as linked entries sharing one journal ID, with the exact same net ledger effect as a single multi-line posting.

Payroll Journal

Enter the figures from your payroll run (or payslip) — this posts DR Salary Expense against CR NI Payable / PAYE Payable / Pension Payable / Net Pay Payable in one linked journal. Net pay is calculated, not entered.
Estimates PAYE and employee/employer NI from gross pay using the rates below — an annualised-equivalent calculation, not a true cumulative payroll history. Fills in the fields below, which stay editable if you already have exact figures from a real payslip or payroll software.
PAYE/NI rates used (edit if these are out of date for the current tax year)

Dividend

Posts DR Retained Earnings against CR Dividends Payable (declared, not yet paid) or CR a bank account (paid immediately). Blocked if the amount exceeds distributable reserves (cumulative net profit, less dividends already posted) — posting an unlawful dividend has real consequences for directors.

Mileage Log

Business trips in your own vehicle, claimed at HMRC's approved mileage rate (45p/mile for the first 10,000 business miles in the tax year, 25p after — flat rates for motorcycle/bicycle). Posts straight to the ledger as a Mileage Expense.

"Run Depreciation" posts one month's charge per registered asset as a normal Dr Depreciation Expense / Cr Accumulated Depreciation transaction — so it flows straight into the P&L and reduces net book value on the Statement of Financial Position.

Revalues an account's foreign-currency transactions at a new rate and posts the GBP difference to Unrealised FX Gain/Loss. Only affects accounts with at least one transaction originally posted as a foreign currency (the "Foreign currency" checkbox on Post Transaction).

P&L by Month

One row per income/expense account, one column per month — the fastest way to spot a trend (a cost creeping up, a sales dip) without opening an accountant's report.

Cash Flow Forecast

Projected cash position: current cash, plus outstanding receivables/payables and recurring journal commitments due within each window. Not a guarantee — customers and suppliers don't always pay on the due date.

What-If Scenarios

Overlay a hypothetical event on the forecast above — e.g. "if I land this £20k invoice in 30 days" — without it touching your real ledger. Cleared on reload.

Tax Computation (estimate)

A working estimate only — uses the Financial Statements period above (Generate that first). Depreciation is added back (it isn't tax-deductible) and capital allowances are deducted instead; enter your own capital allowances figure since this app doesn't track AIA/WDA pools.

CIS Monthly Return

CIS suffered (deductions withheld from your invoices, recoverable against your tax bill) and CIS deducted on bills you've paid to subcontractors (owed to HMRC) — using the Financial Statements period above. Record a CIS deduction when marking an invoice/bill paid.

Self Assessment Helper (SA103)

For sole traders: maps P&L accounts to SA103 self-employment boxes by keyword, using the Financial Statements period above. A starting point for your return, not a substitute for checking each account lands in the right box.

Defaults

Period Lock

Once you've filed a VAT return or finalized accounts up to a date, lock it — transactions on or before that date can no longer be added, edited, or deleted by anyone.
Invited accountants (Comment permission or above) can set this without needing full settings access.

Books Start Date

The date your Opening Balances are "as of" — shown as the brought-forward point on the Cash Flow Statement.

Legal Structure

Drives which features show up: dividends only make sense for a limited company, the SA103 helper only for a sole trader. Choosing "Charity" turns fund accounting on by default (you can still turn it back off below).

Fund Accounting

For charities/nonprofits: tag transactions with a fund (restricted/designated/unrestricted) and get a Statement of Financial Activities. Off by default — turning it on changes nothing else about how this company works.

Cost Centres / Departments

For trading companies: tag transactions with a department (Sales, Operations, HR) and get a P&L broken down by department. Off by default — turning it on changes nothing else about how this company works.

CIS (Construction Industry Scheme)

On automatically if you set up as a construction business — turn it on manually here if you do subcontractor work under a different business type. Off by default for everyone else, since most businesses never see a CIS deduction.

Foreign Currency / FX Revaluation

Shows up automatically once you post a transaction in a foreign currency — turn it on manually if you know you'll need it before you've posted one. Off by default for everyone else.
